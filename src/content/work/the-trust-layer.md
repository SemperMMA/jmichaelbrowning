When a federal agency calls and says the digital front door isn't working, what they
usually mean is that no two parts of the organization can agree on what that door even is.

## The brief

We started where I always start: a quiet audit. No deliverables for the first six weeks.
Just listening. Forty-two stakeholder conversations, structured against a single question —
*what would have to be true for the next launch to ship cleanly?*

> The work that mattered most happened in week three, when we admitted in a room full of
> principals that the strategy on the wall was no longer the strategy we were operating.

## The system we shipped

What we built wasn't a product, it was a coordination layer. A standing weekly forum with a
non-negotiable agenda. A decision log with two columns: *decided* and *still open*. A shared
release calendar that the comms team and the platform team could both edit.

## What I learned

That the most expensive failure inside large organizations is almost never a failure of
intelligence. It's a failure of *interface* between teams who each did their part correctly.
